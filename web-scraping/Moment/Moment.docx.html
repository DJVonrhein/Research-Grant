Lecture video 1

Consider the force F acting on the body as shown. The body is fixed at point O.

What effect does the force have? It has a tendency to rotate the body about point O. This tendency is a manifestation of the moment of the force. The force in this figure has a tendency to rotate the body counterclockwise. By convention, this moment is assigned a positive sign.

If we reverse the force, it has a tendency to rotate the body clockwise. By convention, this moment is assigned a negative sign.

Please note that it is not necessary that the body actually rotate about point O. We are only concerned with the tendency of the force to rotate the body.

The moment of a force about a given point , denoted by  is given by the product of the magnitude of the force and the perpendicular distance, r, of the force from the point.

Thus

The units of the moment in SI units is newton-meter.

The moment of a force about a point can be zero either because the force is zero or because the perpendicular distance of the force about the point about which we are taking moments is zero.

For example, in the figure below, the force F passes through the point O. Hence the perpendicular distance of the force to the point is zero, hence the moment of the force is zero.

The moment of a force is a vector quantity. The formula above gives the magnitude of the moment. To understand the direction, let’s look at a three-dimensional view of the same body.

As before, the force tends to rotate the body in the counterclockwise direction. We use the right hand rule to determine the direction. Stretch out the fingers of your right hand in the direction of the force. Keep your thumb perpendicular to the other fingers. Next curl your fingers around the point about which you are taking moments. The thumb will point in the direction of the moment.

If we change the direction of the force, and use the same principle, we will see the thumb will point in the opposite direction.

The vector formulation of the moment makes this direction more explicit.
